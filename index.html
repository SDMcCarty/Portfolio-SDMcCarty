About Me

When I realized I couldn’t be a tiger when I grew up, I thought about what I wanted to do with my decidedly human life and found I love helping. In high school, I was introduced to coding, and decided to pursue it in university. Being young and dumb, I listened when someone close to me said I’d be too stupid to do it. I graduated with degrees in Advertising and Japanese, then moved to Japan to teach English in a public school. I was in Sendai when the earthquake/tsunami/near-nuclear-disaster happened, and worked tirelessly with fellow staff to run the emergency evacuation site. That experience rekindled my desire to help. After returning, I was lost until I remembered - coding! Computers help people in multiple ways and I wanted in on that helping action. I started my coding journey with Thinkful, and am super excited to see where I’ll go on this crazy journey.

Outside of coding, I enjoy art, writing, video games, and cooking. I am a published artist, and (soon-to-be) published author, and have an undeniable love of spices that has culminated in a spice-off with my roommate’s brother. I still hold the crown, and now go by Spice Queen in one part of the world.
